## Scene 2: Feature Overview - Company Selection (0:15-0:45)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the sidebar showing company selection

**Script**:
"The application starts with a simple but powerful interface. On the left sidebar, you can select which companies you want to analyze. We have eight major tech companies available: Apple, Microsoft, Tesla, NVIDIA, Amazon, Google, Meta, and Intel. You can select multiple companies to compare their performance side by side. Let me select Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla for this demonstration."

## Scene 3: Configuration Options (0:45-1:15)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the sidebar configuration options

**Script**:
"Next, you can choose your analysis time period. Options include 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, 1 year, or 2 years. This flexibility allows you to analyze both short-term trends and long-term performance. Let me select 6 months for this demo.

Below that, you can enable or disable technical indicators. I'll enable both the 20-day and 50-day moving averages, as well as the trading volume analysis. These indicators help identify trends and support resistance levels."

**Action**: Select "6 Months" from the dropdown, check the moving average boxes

---

## Scene 4: Key Metrics Display (1:15-1:45)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the metrics section

**Script**:
"Once you've configured your preferences, the application immediately displays key financial metrics for each selected company. For each stock, you can see:

- The current price and the price change over your selected period
- The 52-week high and low prices
- These metrics are updated in real-time from Yahoo Finance

Looking at our three selected companies, you can immediately see which ones have performed well and which ones have experienced declines. This gives you a quick snapshot of relative performance."

**Action**: Scroll through the metrics cards, pointing out the data

---

## Scene 5: Price Trend Chart (1:45-2:15)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the price trend chart with moving averages

**Script**:
"The first visualization shows the price trends for all selected companies over your chosen time period. Each company has its own colored line, making it easy to compare their trajectories.

Notice the dashed lines overlaid on the price trends—these are the moving averages. The 20-day moving average (dashed line) responds quickly to price changes, while the 50-day moving average (dotted line) provides a smoother, longer-term trend indicator.

When the price is above the moving averages, it suggests an uptrend. When it crosses below, it may indicate a potential downtrend. This is a key technical analysis tool used by professional traders."

**Action**: Hover over the chart to show interactive tooltips, zoom in on a specific time period

---

## Scene 6: Normalized Performance Comparison (2:15-2:45)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the normalized performance chart

**Script**:
"The next chart shows normalized performance comparison. This is particularly useful when comparing stocks with different price levels. All prices are indexed to 100 at the start of the period, so you can see which company has performed best percentage-wise, regardless of their absolute price.

For example, even if one stock is trading at $100 and another at $1000, this chart shows you which one has delivered better returns to investors. This is essential for fair performance comparison."

**Action**: Scroll down to show the normalized chart

---

## Scene 7: Trading Volume Analysis (2:45-3:15)

**Visual**: Screen recording of the volume chart

**Script**:
"The trading volume chart shows how many shares were traded each day for each company. Higher volumes typically indicate stronger market interest and can confirm trend changes.

Notice how volume spikes sometimes coincide with significant price movements. This is important because high-volume price moves are generally considered more significant than low-volume moves in technical analysis."

**Action**: Point out volume spikes on the chart

## Recording Instructions

### Software Setup
- Use screen recording software (OBS Studio, Camtasia, or built-in screen recorder)
- Record at 1080p resolution for clarity
- Use a clear, professional microphone

### Audio Options
1. **Record Your Own Voice**: Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
2. **Use Text-to-Speech**: Services like MiniMax, Google Text-to-Speech, or Amazon Polly can generate professional narration
3. **Add Subtitles**: Include English subtitles for accessibility

### Recording Tips
- Slow down your mouse movements for clarity
- Pause briefly between scenes to allow viewers to absorb information
- Highlight important elements by zooming in or drawing attention
- Use smooth transitions between scenes
- Keep the video between 2-3 minutes as required

### Post-Production
- Edit out any mistakes or long pauses
- Add title slides at the beginning and end
- Include captions for key terms and metrics
- Add background music at low volume (optional)
- Export as MP4 format for compatibility

### Hosting Options
- Upload to YouTube (unlisted or public)
- Upload to GitHub (as a video file or link)
- Host on Streamlit Cloud (embed in app)
- Use any video hosting service (Vimeo, Loom, etc.)
